Portfolio as a garden, not a museum

A personal site does not have to behave like a frozen gallery. It can be a living surface: finished work, rough notes, experiments, references, and questions that are still becoming useful.

That style fits work at the edge of cyber security and AI automation. The field rewards public thinking, careful iteration, and a record of how ideas change over time.

Show the work in motion

Case studies are useful, but so are logs, short articles, failed attempts, and small tools. Together they show taste, judgment, and the ability to learn in public.

Make maintenance natural

A garden stays alive because it is easy to tend. The site should make adding a note or publishing a small article feel ordinary, so the work can keep growing without becoming heavy.
