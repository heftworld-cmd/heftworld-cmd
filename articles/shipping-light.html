Shipping light

Small projects become useful when they are allowed to finish. Shipping light means choosing a narrow result, making it work, and resisting the urge to turn every experiment into a platform.

This is especially helpful with automation and AI experiments. A script that solves one recurring task today can teach more than a large system that never leaves the notebook.

Pick the smallest real outcome

A real outcome changes a workflow. It can be tiny: summarize one report, enrich one alert, rename one messy set of files, or turn one repeated checklist into a reliable command.

Leave room for the next version

Shipping light is not the same as shipping carelessly. It means designing with enough structure that the next version is obvious, while keeping the first version small enough to actually use.
